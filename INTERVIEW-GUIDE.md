## The Process Questions

**Walk me through it step by step.** When a client comes to you for [this topic], what's the very first thing you do? Then what? Walk through the entire process from start to finish as if you're explaining it to a smart new hire on their first day.

**What information do you need before you start?** What must you know from the client before you can begin this part of the planning? What happens if they don't have that information yet?

**What tools/documents/templates do you use?** Any spreadsheets, checklists, software, or documents you always pull out for this stage?

## The Secret Sauce Questions üå∂Ô∏è

**What do less experienced planners always get wrong here?** Think about the mistakes you used to make, or that you see others make. What do you know now that you wish you knew then?

**What's your rule of thumb / benchmark / magic number?** Any percentages, timeframes, ratios, or pricing benchmarks you use as mental shortcuts? (e.g. "Venue should never exceed X% of budget", "Always add X minutes buffer for Y")

**What do you always check that others forget?** The thing you've learned the hard way to look for. The question you always ask that surprises clients or vendors.

**What's your negotiation or communication tactic here?** How do you handle the tricky conversations ‚Äî pricing pushback, difficult clients, vendor issues? What's your actual language or approach?

**What are the regional/seasonal patterns you've noticed?** Things that are specific to the areas you work in, or patterns that shift with seasons that someone new wouldn't know.

---

## The Real-World Questions

**Give me an example of when this went perfectly.** A real scenario (names changed) where your process worked exactly as it should. What made it work?

**Give me an example of when this went wrong.** A real scenario where something unexpected happened. What went wrong, how did you recover, and what did you change in your process because of it?

**What are the top 3 things that can go wrong at this stage?** And what's your playbook for each one?

---

## The Decision Tree Questions

**When do you say no / redirect / escalate?** Are there situations where you handle this differently? Red flags that change your approach? Thresholds where you bring in extra help?

**How does this change based on the type of wedding?** Think about how your approach differs for: intimate vs. large, budget-conscious vs. luxury, local vs. destination, cultural/religious considerations, weekday vs. weekend.

---

## How to Use This Guide

You have two options:

**Option A (Solo):** Read each question, write your answers. Don't overthink it. Spend 20-30 minutes per skill.

**Option B (Interview Style):** Have someone ask you these questions out loud while you talk through your answers. Record it or have them take notes. This is usually faster and more natural ‚Äî you'll say things you wouldn't think to write.

Once you have your answers, open **[templates/TEMPLATE.md](./templates/TEMPLATE.md)** and organise them into the skill format.
